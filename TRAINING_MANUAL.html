1. System Overview

The Deaf AI Healthcare Assistant translates hand gestures into medical words in real-time using your camera. It uses two AI models together:

ModelTypeWhat It DetectsAccuracy
Google GestureRecognizer ML MODEL Pre-trained neural network 7 core gestures: Open Palm, Fist, Thumbs Up, Thumbs Down, Peace/Victory, Pointing Up, ILY High
Custom Geometric Classifier RULE-BASED Finger angle analysis 8 extra medical gestures: Pinch, OK Sign, Horns, Call Me, Three Up, L-Shape, Palm Down, Four Up Good

The ML model runs first as the primary detector. The geometric classifier adds extra medical gesture support that the ML model doesn't cover.

2. How the System Detects Signs

Detection Pipeline

1
Camera captures your hand — Keep your hand clearly visible, well-lit
2
ML model checks for gesture — Google's neural network analyzes the image
3
Geometric classifier runs — Checks for additional medical gestures the ML model doesn't know
4
Stability check — You must hold the gesture steady for ~0.4 seconds (2 frames) before it registers
5
Word appears on screen — The gesture is translated to a standard SASL medical word (same in every department)
💡 Anti-Repetition: The same word will NOT repeat. To say the same word again, you must first make a different gesture, or put your hand down and raise it again. A word cannot re-appear within 5 seconds.

These 15 gestures work the same in every department. Each gesture always means the same word — following South African Sign Language (SASL) conventions. No confusion, no department-specific overrides.

4. Technique: How to Sign Clearly

✅ DO

🎯Hold each gesture for at least 1 second — the system needs 2 consecutive frames (~0.4s) to register
💡Use good lighting — face a window or lamp; avoid backlight behind your hand
📏Keep hand 30–60 cm from camera — not too close, not too far
🖐️Exaggerate finger positions — spread fingers wide for open palm; curl tightly for fist
⏸️Pause between gestures — drop hand briefly or make a neutral position between words
🎨Plain background — avoid busy patterns behind your hand

❌ DON'T

🚫Don't switch gestures too quickly — transitioning fast causes false detections
🚫Don't wave your hand around — keep it steady while holding a gesture
🚫Don't show both hands — the system tracks one hand only
🚫Don't hold the same gesture expecting repeated words — each word requires: gesture → pause → gesture
⚠️ To say the same word twice: Show gesture → put hand down (or make a different gesture) → show original gesture again. Simply holding a gesture will NOT repeat the word.

8. Detection Modes

The translator supports three modes, switchable via buttons:

ModeButtonUse ForHow It Works
Words (Glosses) 🤟 Words Main communication — medical phrases Shows gestures → standard SASL words. Uses ML + geometric models.
Letters 🔤 ABC Spelling names, medications, specific words ASL fingerspelling A–Z. Requires holding each letter 0.8s.
Numbers 🔢 123 Pain scale (1–10), room numbers, quantities ASL number signs 0–9.

When to Use Each Mode

  • Start with Words mode — for symptoms, requests, responses
  • Switch to Letters — to spell a medication name, doctor's name, or unfamiliar word
  • Switch to Numbers — when asked "rate your pain 1–10" or for dosages

9. Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Gesture not detectedBetter lighting, hold steadier, keep hand 30–60 cm from camera
Wrong word detectedExaggerate the gesture more — spread fingers wider, curl tighter
System shows "Collecting..."Your gesture hasn't been stable for 2 frames yet — hold still
Same word won't repeatPut hand down, wait 1 second, show gesture again
"No hand" messageMove hand into the camera frame; ensure good lighting
Gesture words too generalUse Quick Phrase buttons for specific symptoms (e.g. "Cannot breathe", "Blurry vision")
Camera not workingAllow camera permissions in browser, check if another app is using the camera
